information delivery: thanksgiving 2012

 

Our final event of 2012, was a new type of affair. Instead of a day-long competition, we tried out our latest format - condensed into a single evening, hosted at a VC's offices in Manhattan. For our first event, we chose ff Venture Capital in midtown.

for more photos, check out the event album on facebook.

politics: fall 2012

 

September saw our second official event, and what a day it was! Some fifteen teams vied for the best business idea within the theme of politics, in honor of the recent presidental elections.

for more photos, check out the event album on facebook.

 

changes & improvements

some of the teams

To augment the scoring by mentor investment, we added in the concept of user voting. Each competitor could vote for up to three other teams per round. Scores were derived from the investment by mentors, and the votes they accumulated from the competitors.


count me in: simplifying voter registrations

We also introduced the idea of video submissions into round 3. The top eight teams had sixty minutes to create a sixty second clip! if you've ever tried it, you'll know it's no mean feat.


campaign.ly: instant polling feedback and analytics

Finally, to direct the flow of pitches, elimation was introduced later in the afternoon. those knocked out were able to form into new teams of one - four competitors and could pitch in the re-tilt. Congratulations to our re-tilt winners michael and evan with their concept super roots.


politerate: political trivia & donations application

rentals: spring 2012

What a day! The first official event ran in April and we had a myriad of impressive business concepts emerge around the theme of improving the nyc rental market. The six teams - aviator glasses, arthur, mcduck, friend for hire, jope and folka.ly - created some incredible ideas, ranging from hyperlocal reviews of apartments, buildings, blocks and surrounds, to a syndicate of high-end musical equipment owners, interested in renting out their gear for cash.

for more photos, check out our album on facebook.

Congratulations to our winning team - jope - and their idea of a citywide rental trust. Jon, Ordan, Pierre and Eric (yes, that spells j-o-p-e), we hope you enjoy your kindle touches - well earned.

Thanks again to everyone who attended, especially to our investors Ryan, Ritesh, Jon and Anand. And once again, thanks to the brewheister Sam Burlingame, and his incredible brew - it certainly went down a treat.
