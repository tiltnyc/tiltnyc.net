politics: fall 2012

late september saw our second official event, and what a day it was! some fifteen-odd teams vied for the best business idea within the theme of politics, in honor of the recent presidental elections.

for more photos, check out the event album on facebook.

improvements

to augment the scoring by mentor investment, we added in the concept of user voting. each competitor could vote for up to three other teams per round. scores were derived from the investment by mentors, and the votes they accumulated from the competitors.
for some variety, we introduced the idea of video submissions into round 3. the top eight teams had sixty minutes to create a sixty second clip! if you've ever tried it, you'll know it's no mean feat.

we also introduced elimation later in the afternoon. those knocked out were able to form into new teams of one - four competitors and could pitch in the re-tilt. congratulations to our re-tilt winners michael and evan with their concept super roots.

example teams

as always, the day drew some varied business proposals. some of the ideas included:

countmein
a system to simplify voter registrations

campaign.ly
instant polling feedback and analytics app

politerate
a political trivia & donations app

results

after four full rounds of pitching, pivoting, networking, sketching, shooting and filming, our winners emerged.

instagram

first up, was our prize for best instagram photo. thanks to everyone for submitting plenty of great entries throughout the day.

but alas, there can be only one winner. congratulations to camille - aka @sitinpretty - for that killer shot of team nudge. hope you enjoy your roku player!

winning mentor

like before, our mentors were competing for the strongest portfolio after the final round. unlike last time, two judges joined in the final round - charlie and erik - who, with their larger allocation of virtual capital, primarily determined the final winners. and thus, the mentor who got the highest return on investment.

congratulations to will peng from raptor ventures!

runners up

our runners up were the team who designed a system that matches users interested in a particular issue with lobbyists who will take up the cause.

lobbyist.me are a kickstarter for causes. checkout their product video. well done!

and, our winners...

nudge, aka team champaign, are a group that want to give you quantifiable actions for issues that matter to you.

check out nudge and their product video on our live site. congrats guys!
